Program Notes

The Ritz Chamber Players present programs that are thought provoking, inspiring and relevant, showing the power of classical music to express the complexity of our time. Chamber music in particular often reveals a shared perspective because of its inherent intimacy. We hope you enjoy these performances.

In Remembrance of The Dream

Ties That Bind: From Swastika to Jim Crow The Ritz Chamber Players are excited to present a concert performance of works that express the “Ties That Bind” the African-American and Jewish communities in the United States. To an extent, African-American and Jewish people came into contact through the Atlantic Slave Trade during the seventeenth and eighteenth centuries, but the crux of their relationship has been largely ideological, as the two groups bonded as fellow minorities. For their part, African-Americans sympathized and related to the history of the Jewish people facing oppression in both Egypt and Europe. African-American spirituals and slave songs such as “Go Down, Moses” reflect these ties, as you will hear within the stunning work by award-winning living Black composer Adulphus HAILSTORK in his American Landscape No. 2 for Violin and Cello.

Tragedy Toward Peace

For our Black History Month Performance the Ritz Chamber Players share this thought provoking program that commemorates the violet gun deaths of so many of our African-American youth. Gun violence in our communities, police brutality and racial profiling—these are not just “black issues” or “African-American issues,” they are American issues. This tribute concert is marked by a performance of the remarkable work String Quartet No.1 based on the spiritual “Calvary” by celebrated African-American composer Coleridge-Taylor PERKINSON written when he was 17 years old! As we take the time to remember the tragic death of each of these young men, we leave you with a work of peace and hope! Lee JOHNSON’s “Infinitude” has the spoken/narrated text of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights that begins with: Whereas recognition of the inherent dignity and of the equal and inalienable rights of all members of the human family is the foundation of freedom, justice and peace in the world. PERKINSON String Quartet No.1 based on the spiritual “Calvary” JOHNSON “Infinitude”

Education and Outreach

Providing valuable community resources is vital to our mission! The Ritz Chamber Players Education Initiatives introduce children to classical music and the concert-going experience; and foster a lasting and meaningful relationship between the arts and the community. Through our Community Initiatives, the Ritz Chamber Players aid students in the increase of their knowledge of the arts, culture, as well as create and sustain an environment that supports intellectual inquiry, cultural enhancement, and awareness of broader issues.

Scholarships

The Mini Ritz Conservatory provides complete funding to send minority students to the Prelude Chamber Music Camp. The program offers young musicians the opportunity to experience chamber music by participating and performing in small ensembles with guidance and training provided by professional musicians. The Prelude Chamber Music Camp was founded in 2002 by Jeanne Majors, violinist with the Jacksonville Symphony Orchestra, and is held at Hendricks Avenue Baptist Church during the first two weeks of June. For more information, take a look at preludechambermusic.org

In the future, we hope to expand this program to support minority students at other music camps around the country.

Elderly Outreach

The Ritz Chamber Players recognizes the importance of outreach efforts in the elderly community with special performances at the NBA Cypress Village and Vicar’s Landing.

In addition to bringing our musicians into these senior centers, the Ritz Chamber Players often seeks to arrange special transportation and catering to help bring these seniors to our Concert Series at the Times-Union Center for the Performing Arts to enjoy a wonderful evening of art and entertainment.

Educational Activities

In-School Performances/Informances: A series of daytime concerts designed to introduce major chamber music works and composers of classical music to students and teachers and share our knowledge and enthusiasm for music with aspiring musicians.

Music Mentor Program/In-School Coachings: Members of the Ritz Chamber Players provide the instrumental students at Butler with mini-lessons, chamber coachings, and sectional rehearsals.

RCP Open Rehearsals: Throughout the season students are welcome to observe the Ritz Chamber Players in rehearsal. This provides a better understanding of the music-making process and the amount of time and energy required to create a first-class performance. Students also have the opportunity to ask questions and interact with the musicians.

Masterclasses

There simply are no better opportunities for serious students of classical music to improve than to spend time with professionals who have mastered their instruments. The schedule is to be announced. Previously, we had great reception for Masterclasses by: Terrence Wilson on Piano, and Tahirah Whittington on Cello

Terrance Patterson

A Jacksonville, Florida, native, Terrance Patterson has performed in Paris, London, Milan, Brussels, Belgrade, Munich, Amsterdam, Moscow, Washington, D.C., Baltimore, Miami, Philadelphia, Los Angeles, and New York. He has performed with the Jacksonville Symphony Orchestra, the Sphinx Symphony of Detroit and the Nashville, Florida West Coast, Huntsville, Festival, and Las Vegas Symphonies. He attended the Peabody Conservatory of the Johns Hopkins University where he studied with clarinetist Lorin Kitt, principal clarinetist of the National Symphony Orchestra.

Ann Hobson Pilot

Ann Hobson Pilot is a graduate of the Cleveland Institute of Music. She became principal harp of the Boston Symphony Orchestra in 1980, having joined the BSO in 1969 as assistant principal harp and principal harp with the Boston Pops. Previously she was substitute second harp with the Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra and principal harp of Washington, D.C.’s National Symphony Orchestra. Ms. Pilot also has had an extensive solo career; she has performed as a soloist with many American orchestras, as well as with orchestras in Europe, Haiti, New Zealand, and South Africa.
